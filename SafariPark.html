Safari Park - Karachi's Wild Escape into Nature

Nestled in the heart of Karachi, Safari Park is a sprawling urban wildlife sanctuary where adventure meets conservation. Home to a wide variety of animals and birds, this park offers families and nature lovers a unique chance to explore the wild without leaving the city. From scenic landscapes to up-close animal encounters, Safari Park is a true heaven for wildlife enthusiasts.

Safari Ride Experience

Guided rides through enclosures featuring lions, deer, and other wildlife in natural settings.

Diverse Bird Aviaries

Aviaries showcasing diverse bird species, including parrots, peacocks, and regional exotics.

Learning & Leisure

Facilities for educational tours, guided visits, and family recreation.

Meet the Wildlife of Safari Park

Lion

The lion is a large, muscular cat known for its golden coat and majestic mane. It's often called the "king of the jungle."

  • Scientific Name: Panthera leo
  • Habitat: Grassland, woodland of Africa.
  • Population in Park: 3 individuals

Macaw

Macaws are large, colorful parrots known for their intelligence, strong beaks, and loud calls.

  • Scientific Name: Ara macao
  • Habitat: Rainforests & woodlands
  • Population in Park: 9 individuals

Elephant

The elephant is the largest land animal, known for its long trunk, tusks, and large ears.

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta africana
  • Habitat: Forests, grassland in Africa, Asia
  • Population in Park: 4 individuals

Ostrich

The ostrich is the largest and heaviest bird, flightless but incredibly fast on land, with powerful legs.

  • Scientific Name: Struthio camelus
  • Habitat: Savannas & plains of Africa.
  • Population in Park: 7 individuals

Giraffe

The giraffe is the tallest land animal, recognized by its long neck, long legs, and spotted coat.

  • Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis
  • Habitat: Woodland, savannas of Africa.
  • Population in Park: 5 individuals

Flamingo

Flamingos are wading birds with long legs and necks, famous for their pink coloration and group behavior.

  • Scientific Name: Phoenicopterus roseus
  • Habitat: lakes, lagoons, and wetlands
  • Population in Park: 8 individuals
