Lalsuhanra Park - Wild Beauty of Bahawalpur

Located on the edge of the Cholistan Desert, Lalsuhanra National Park is one of Pakistan's largest and most diverse parks. Spanning lush forests, arid plains, and freshwater lakes, the park offers a rare combination of ecosystems that support a wide range of wildlife. It's a haven for nature lovers, birdwatchers, and those seeking peaceful landscapes in Southern Punjab.

Diverse Habitats

Lalsuhanra boasts an extraordinary mix of desert, forest, and wetland areas, making it a unique sanctuary for a wide variety of plant and animal species.

Home to Rare Wildlife

The park is home to endangered species like the Chinkara gazelle and various desert reptiles, along with hundreds of native and migratory birds.

Nature & Fun

Visitors can enjoy birdwatching, camel rides, nature walks, and photography in a peaceful natural setting.

Meet the Wildlife of LalSuhanra Park

Crocodile

Crocodiles are large aquatic reptiles with powerful jaws, rough skin, and a prehistoric look.

  • Scientific Name: Crocodylus niloticus
  • Habitat: Rivers, lakes & wetlands
  • Population in Park: 9 individuals

Eagle

Eagles are powerful birds of prey with sharp talons, keen eyesight, and impressive wingspans.

  • Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus
  • Habitat: Forests, mountains in America
  • Population in Park: 8 individuals

Tiger

The tiger is the largest species of big cat, known for its orange coat with black stripes and powerful build.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands & wetlands.
  • Population in Park: 6 individuals

Parrot

Parrots are brightly colored, highly intelligent birds known for their ability to mimic sounds and human speech.

  • Scientific Name: Psittacus erithacus
  • Habitat: Rainforests, savanna, woodland
  • Population in Park: 15 individuals

Zebra

Zebras are known for their distinctive black and white stripes and are related to horses.

  • Scientific Name: Equus quagga
  • Habitat: Grasslands & woodlands
  • Population in Park: 10 individuals

Owl

Owls are nocturnal birds of prey with silent flight, forward-facing eyes, and excellent night vision.

  • Scientific Name: Bubo bubo
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands, mountains
  • Population in Park: 11 individuals
